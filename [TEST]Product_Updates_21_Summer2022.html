The latest product updates

New layouts, more chart types, and global data

Rest is a strategy and the summer months are ideal for recharging. As you ease back into markets, and return to your trading desk, our team created new tools that await your return. Here’s what’s new.
Add images to your chart
Whether it’s a screenshot of financial data, your business logo or a meme, you can now place that photo directly on your charts. Our new image feature creates infinite possibilities for the things that can be added to your chart.
How to copy + paste
 
New chart types: High-Low and Columns
We created two new chart types so you can visualize price in more unique ways. High-Low shows the high and low for a trading period while Columns does exactly what it sounds like - it turns the chart into columns.
 
Try High-Low charts
 
New chart layouts: 2x3 and more
All members can now access several new layouts including 2x3, 5 across, 6 across, 7 across, and 8 across. These layouts increase the number of ways you can research multiple symbols at once.
 
See the new layouts
 
More brokers, more data
We are thrilled to welcome Interactive Brokers, Eightcap, OKX, Dorman Trading, City Index, and Tradier to our list of connected brokers. Connect your brokerage account to your TradingView account and begin trading right from the chart.
Alongside these new broker connections, there are also new data sources from Uniswap, MEXC and Abu Dhabi Securities Exchange. Yep - you read that right - all TradingView members now have access to data that spans the world, including the second largest exchange in the Middle East.
 
Pine Script™ enhancements
We introduced a new type of behavior for your Pine scripts which allows them to recalculate and redraw on visible bars, automatically readjusting when traders scroll or zoom on their chart.
 
Learn dynamic recalculation
